1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

1.1 Business Overview

  • Business Name: Reid's Drop
  • Umbrella Company: Tui & Tide Beverages LTD
  • Business Structure: Limited Liability Company (LLC) with Co-Ownership
  • Industry: Craft Beer and Flavored Lager Production

Sales Pitch

Are you tired of the same old boring, bland-tasting beer? Reid's Drop incorporates a multitude of cultures and centuries of beer-making techniques and innovations to create the smoothest and crispest beer on the market.

Business Development Strategy

  • Start with small batches for friends and family testing and review
  • Expand to offering samples to strangers for broader feedback
  • Obtain proper licensing and regulatory compliance
  • Begin sales at farmers markets to establish market presence

1.2 Key Personnel

Founders

  • Bruce Reid
  • Mackenzie Reid
  • Tomoki Huskinson

Key Stakeholders

  • Bruce Reid (Primary Stakeholder)
  • Potential Investors: Bruce Reid and/or Robb Huskinson

1.3 Mission & Vision

Mission Statement

To provide an exceptionally refreshing alternative to mundane, bland beers and lagers.

Business Description

Tui & Tide Beverages LTD serves as the umbrella company for Reid's Drop. Reid's Drop is a specialized business that focuses on craft beer and flavored lagers. Created by students for students, Reid's Drop aims to introduce the target market to the world of craft beer and flavored lager without the bitter, earthy taste of classic lagers.

1.4 Potential Selling Positions

Primary Venues

  • Bar101: Student-owned and focused bar selling various affordable drinks to students (open Wednesday, Friday, Saturday, and Sundays preceding public holidays)
  • Student Halls: Ideal selling position given co-owners' current living situation; residents frequent Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday outings
  • Shadows: Student bar allowing word-of-mouth marketing among student demographic

Expansion Venues

  • Music Festivals: Large demographic with mature taste preferences (ideal for IPA enthusiasts)
  • Golf Courses: Premium market segment
  • Retirement Homes: Specialized market with specific preferences
  • Reggae/Hippie Vibe Festivals: Cultural alignment with brand values

Major Retail Opportunities

  • Liquor Stores: Enable mass selling with additional packaging requirements (6-packs, 12-packs)
  • Supermarkets: Cheap mass sales potential but challenging entry requirements and extreme restrictions
  • Private Sales: Office functions and house parties

1.5 Target Market

Primary Demographic: University students ages 18-30 who may not appreciate the taste of classic lagers but prefer flavored lagers or craft beers.

2. COMPANY DESCRIPTION

2.1 Legal Structure and Ownership

  • Structure: Limited Liability Company (LLC)
  • Ownership Split: 50/50 between Mackenzie Reid and Tomoki Huskinson

LLC Operational Framework

Ownership Structure

  • LLCs do not issue shares; owners are called members with percentage-based membership interests
  • Ownership percentages can be based on contributions (money, assets, time) or agreed-upon arrangements

Management Style

  • Member-Managed LLC: All members involved in business operations with voting power based on ownership percentage
  • Manager-Managed LLC: Members choose managers for daily operations; members vote only on major decisions

Decision-Making Framework

  • Majority vote required for large decisions (branding, partnerships, etc.)
  • 2/3 majority decision threshold for significant operational changes
  • Operating Agreement controls decision-making authority and voting requirements

2.2 Current Business Stage

  • Current Stage: Research & Development / Startup Phase
  • Long-Term Vision: Distribution across all sales channels including bars, alcohol stores, and supermarkets throughout New Zealand

2.3 Unique Value Proposition

  • Provision of flavored IPAs targeting students and young adults
  • Cultural integration of centuries-old brewing techniques with modern preferences
  • Student-focused brand created by students for authentic market understanding
  • Introduction to craft beer without traditional bitter, earthy taste barriers

5. PRODUCTS OR SERVICES

5.1 Product Description

Reid's Drop specializes in craft beer and flavored lagers designed specifically for the student market. Our products bridge the gap between traditional lagers and craft beers, offering approachable flavors without the bitter, earthy taste that often deters younger consumers.

5.2 Key Features and Benefits

  • Smooth and Crisp Taste: Incorporates multiple cultural brewing techniques for optimal flavor
  • Student-Focused Flavors: Designed to appeal to 18-30 age demographic
  • Premium Quality: Craft brewing standards with accessible pricing
  • Cultural Integration: Centuries of beer-making traditions modernized for contemporary palates

5.3 Development Stage

Current Phase: Research & Development with small batch testing among friends and family

Next Steps: Expand testing to broader demographic, obtain licensing, and begin farmers market sales

5.4 Package Design Considerations

Bottle Cap Options

  • Twist-off Caps: More expensive but offer customer convenience
  • Pop-off Caps: Traditional, cost-effective option widely accepted for beer
  • Recommendation: Start with pop-off caps to minimize costs, upgrade to twist-off as scale increases

5.5 Future Product Lines

  • Canned Varieties: Cost-effective, lightweight option for broader distribution
  • Seasonal Flavors: Limited edition varieties for different times of year
  • Premium Line: Higher-end craft options for mature market segment

Decision-Making Framework

6.2 Operational Plan

Production Partnership Model

Reid's Drop will operate through partnerships with established breweries rather than maintaining our own production facilities. This approach reduces startup costs and leverages existing expertise.

Quality Control Process

  • Recipe Development: Collaborative process with brewery partners
  • Batch Testing: Small-scale trials before full production
  • Feedback Integration: Continuous improvement based on customer input
  • Consistency Monitoring: Regular quality checks across all batches

Supply Chain Management

  • Brewery Partnerships: Multiple suppliers to ensure reliability
  • Inventory Management: Just-in-time approach to minimize storage costs
  • Distribution Network: Graduated expansion from local to regional

6.3 Technology and Tools

  • Inventory Tracking: Simple spreadsheet-based system initially
  • Customer Relationship Management: Direct communication through social platforms
  • Financial Management: Basic accounting software for tracking expenses and revenue
